🌿 Steps to Cultivate Millets 🌿

1. Land Preparation 🌱

Plough and level the land, remove weeds, and apply compost or FYM to enrich soil fertility.

2. Seed Selection 🌾

Choose certified, region-suitable millet seeds that are disease-resistant and high-yielding.

3. Seed Treatment 💊

Treat seeds with bio-fungicides or organic solutions to prevent early-stage infections.

4. Sowing 🌿

Use broadcasting or line sowing with proper spacing; sow during monsoon for best results.

5. Irrigation 💧

Millets require minimal water. Irrigate only during flowering and grain filling stages.

6. Weeding 🌾

Perform manual or tool-based weeding 2–3 times. Mulching can also help prevent weeds.

7. Fertilization 🌿

Apply organic fertilizers like vermicompost. Use small doses of N and P if needed.

8. Pest & Disease Control 🐛

Millets are hardy, but monitor for pests like shoot fly. Use neem-based sprays if required.

9. Harvesting 🧺

Harvest when grains are dry and hard, usually 2.5–4 months after sowing.

10. Post-Harvest Storage 🏡

Dry grains thoroughly and store in clean, dry, airtight containers or bags.
